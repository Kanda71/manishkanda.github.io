Visual Notes

A collection of experiments, field work, and observations.

Laboratory Setup: Optics Bench

This was the setup for the laser diffraction experiment. We used a standard He-Ne laser to observe the interference patterns created by the single slit. Aligning the mirrors took nearly two hours, but the resulting fringe definition was worth the effort.

Deriving the Hamiltonian

Snapshot of my notebook during a late-night study session. I was trying to work through the perturbations for the quantum harmonic oscillator. There is something satisfying about writing these equations out by hand rather than typing them in LaTeX.

Circuit Prototyping

A close-up of the PCB we designed for the signal processing unit. You can see the traces connecting the op-amps to the filter capacitors. We ran into some noise issues on the ground plane, which we had to debug using an oscilloscope.

Field Work in the Himalayas

Taken during our survey of the Mandi region. We were collecting soil samples to analyze the stability of the slopes. The terrain here is incredibly rugged, making data collection physically demanding but visually stunning.
