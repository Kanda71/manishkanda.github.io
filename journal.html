The Art of Scientific Explanation

Why do students struggle with electromagnetism? I've been reflecting on teaching physics with clarity. Using analogies is helpful, but visual intuition is paramount. In my recent demo class, I found that starting with the phenomena before the math increased engagement significantly. We often get lost in the equations and forget to show the beauty of the physical world first.

September 22, 2025

Digital Minimalism in Science

In an era of constant notifications, I explore why clean interfaces and distraction-free writing environments are essential for the deep thinking required in theoretical physics. I have recently stripped my digital workspace down to the bare essentials: a text editor, a terminal, and a PDF reader. The clarity of mind that follows is astonishing.

August 10, 2025

Late Nights in the Lab

There is a specific kind of silence that exists in a physics lab at 2 AM. The hum of the cooling fans and the faint glow of the oscilloscope are the only company. Tonight, we finally stabilized the laser output for the diffraction experiment. Progress is slow, but these small victories keep the passion alive.
